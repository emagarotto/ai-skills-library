When asked to research a company, generate a meeting brief, or produce a company overview, follow this structure:

Pull from: company website, SEC filings, Crunchbase, LinkedIn, earnings transcripts, WSJ/Bloomberg/TechCrunch, Glassdoor, G2/Gartner.

Write all sections in clear prose. Cite sources inline. State what is unknown rather than speculating.

Sections (in order):
- Executive Summary (written last, 3-5 sentences on stage, top strength, key risk)
- Company Overview (what it does, HQ, founding date, current stage, core problem solved)
- Founders and Leadership Team (pre-company backgrounds, motivations, current executives, leadership changes)
- Product Lines and Revenue Breakdown (full portfolio, positioning, differentiators, revenue by segment, pricing)
- Major Customers and Case Studies (named customers, verticals served, case study outcomes, concentration risks)
- Business Model and Revenue Sources (all revenue streams, pricing model, margins if available)
- Market Size and Competitive Positioning (TAM/SAM, main competitors, positioning, moats)
- Current Strategic Goals and Priorities (from earnings calls, investor days, and annual reports)
- Hiring Signals (job posting analysis revealing investment areas and geographic expansion)
- Future Growth Plans and Market Expansion (roadmap, new markets, M&A history and signals)
- Funding History and Financials (all rounds with amounts, dates, lead investors, valuation, revenue metrics)
- Risks, Open Questions, and Red Flags (regulatory, competitive, legal, leadership, and concentration risks)
- Recent News and Traction (5-7 developments from the past 6-12 months with dates)
- SWOT Analysis (3-5 items per quadrant: Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats)
- Sources (every URL or document referenced)

Format output as structured markdown. Do not use bullet points in prose sections. Do not speculate when data is unavailable; state the gap and move on.
